Good Ways to Make Audio and Video Content Accessible

Multimedia is essential for modern web content. Learn how to ensure everyone can access it, regardless of ability or environment.

Why It Matters

Making your audio and video content accessible isn't just a nice-to-have, it's essential for reaching a wider audience. A video is not just about visuals but also audio.

Captions vs. Subtitles

Captions: Provide a text version of spoken words and important non-verbal sounds (like music or laughter), synchronized with the video. Crucial for deaf or hard-of-hearing users.
Subtitles: Essential for people who don't understand the spoken language. They help not only deaf users but also those watching in noisy or quiet environments.

Adding Captions with the <track> Element

To add specific text tracks to your media, use the <track> element nested inside <video> or <audio>.

Video Example

<video
  width="400"
  height="300"
  controls
  src="https://cdn.freecodecamp.org/curriculum/labs/what-is-the-map-method-and-how-does-it-work.mp4"
>
  
  <track
    src="captions.vtt"
    kind="captions"
    srclang="en"
    label="English"
  />
</video>

Audio Example

<audio controls src="sample.mp3">
  <track
    src="captions.vtt"
    kind="captions"
    srclang="en"
    label="English"
  />
</audio>

Attributes Explained

  • src: The URL of the text track file (usually WebVTT format).
  • kind: Specifies the type of text track. Valid values: captions, subtitles, chapters, metadata.
  • srclang: Language of the track text (e.g., "en" for English).
  • label: A user-readable title for the track, shown in the player's menu.

Transcripts

A transcript is a text version of all spoken words. Unlike captions, they don't need to be synchronized. They are vital for users who are deaf-blind (using Braille displays), or anyone who prefers reading.

Transcripts also make your content searchable.

Example Structure

You can place the transcript directly below the media player:

<audio controls>
  <source src="audio.mp3" />
  Your browser does not support the audio element.
</audio>

<!-- Transcript Section -->
<h3>Transcript</h3>
<p>
  [Speaker 1]: Welcome to the tutorial on making accessible content
</p>
<p>
  [Speaker 2]: Today, we'll cover captions, transcripts, and more.
</p>

Pro Tip: Platforms like YouTube provide auto-captions, but customized services like veed.io, Rev, Amara, and Descript offer higher accuracy.

Other Best Practices

  • Sign Language Overlay: For users who communicate primarily via sign language.
  • User Controls: Ensure users can control volume and playback speed.
  • Contrast: Ensure any text displayed inside the video has good contrast against the background.
  • Multiple Formats: Offer the content in various formats if possible.
