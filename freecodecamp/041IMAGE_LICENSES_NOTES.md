# Image Licenses and Copyright

## Overview
Images are **intellectual property** protected by copyright regulations. Understanding licenses is essential before using images on your website.

---

## Default Status: All Rights Reserved
By default, images are released as **all rights reserved**, meaning:
- The creator or publisher holds all copyright
- You **cannot use them** without permission

---

## How to Use Copyrighted Images

### Option 1: Obtain Written Permission
- Contact the copyright holder directly
- Request permission to use the image

### Option 2: Purchase a License
- Pay the copyright holder for usage rights
- Rights depend on the license purchased

### Option 3: Fair Use
⚠️ **Tricky and limited** - Requires:
- Limited use
- Transformative use

**Examples of fair use:**
- Commenting on or reviewing the art
- Creating a parody of the image

---

## Permissive Licenses

### Creative Commons License
- Images available for website use
- **Must follow specific rules** - check the license terms
- Examples:
  - May require your website to be open source
  - May prohibit modifications to the image
  - Creative Commons 0 → public domain

### BSD License
- Similar to Creative Commons
- Used by freeCodeCamp
- Check license terms before use

---

## Public Domain

### What is Public Domain?
- No copyright attached
- **Free to use** without restrictions
- No permission needed
- No license required

### Creative Commons 0
- Specifically licenses images as public domain
- Completely free to use

---

## Free Image Resources

### Search Engine Filtering
- Most search engines allow filtering by license
- Look for permissive or public domain options

### Popular Free Image Sites
- **Pixabay** - Free-to-use images
- **Unsplash** - Free-to-use images

---

## Key Takeaway

✅ **Always check copyright and licensing** before using images on your website.

| Type | Permission Needed | Cost | Rules |
|------|-------------------|------|-------|
| All Rights Reserved | ✅ Yes | May apply | Strict |
| Fair Use | ❌ No* | No | Limited & transformative |
| Creative Commons | ❌ No | No | Follow license terms |
| Public Domain | ❌ No | No | None |

*Fair use is complex and requires legal judgment
