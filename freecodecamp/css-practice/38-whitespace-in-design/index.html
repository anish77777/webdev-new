Importance of Whitespace

White space refers to any type of space around elements like images, text, and buttons. It is vital for balance and clarity.

Room to Breathe

White space is important in design because it helps to create a balance between the elements on the page. Without it, layouts feel cluttered, confusing, and overwhelming for users. Proper spacing makes content pop and guides the viewer's journey.

Call-to-Action Spacing

On the freeCodeCamp homepage, the CTA button is visually separated from other elements. By using white space effectively, we can help to make a CTA button more prominent and encourage users to click on it.

.cta-container { margin: 40px 0; text-align: center; }

Macro & Micro Whitespace

Macro white space is the space between larger elements like images, text blocks, and buttons.

Micro white space is the space between individual characters (kerning/letter-spacing). This improves readability and legibility, making content easier to scan.

.faq-text { letter-spacing: 1px; }
This is an example of Frequently Asked Questions text. Proper micro whitespace allows you to read each question and answer easily.

Active & Passive Whitespace

Active white space is intentionally created to guide the user's eye and draw attention to certain elements.

Passive white space is the space that is left over after all elements have been placed (e.g., margins around the outside of a grid).

The Law of Proximity

This law states that elements that are close together are perceived as being related, while elements that are far apart are perceived as being unrelated.
