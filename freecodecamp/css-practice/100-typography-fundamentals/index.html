1. Typefaces vs Fonts

Typography is the art of choosing the right fonts and format to make text visually appealing. "Type" refers to how individual characters are designed and arranged.

Difference

  • Typeface: The overall design (e.g., Times New Roman). It's the blueprint.
  • Font: A specific variation (e.g., Times New Roman Bold, 12pt). It's the technical implementation.

2. Serif vs Sans Serif

These are the two primary classifications of typefaces.

Serif

Classical style with small lines (serifs) at the ends of characters. Often used for print.

Sans Serif

3. Anatomy of Type

Fundamental elements that define character relationships.

Baseline Hyg

Key Terms

  • Baseline: The imaginary line where characters rest.
  • Cap Height: Height of uppercase letters.
  • X-height: Height of lowercase letters (like 'x').
  • Ascenders: Parts that go above x-height (h, b, d).
  • Descenders: Parts that go below the baseline (y, g, p).
